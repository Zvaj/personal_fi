# Concept Taxonomy for Personal Finance for Young Adults (2025-2026)

This taxonomy organizes the 205 concepts into 22 categories for visualization and navigation. Each category maps to one of the 22 chapters.

## Categories

### 1. Foundation Concepts (FOUND)

Core prerequisite concepts that serve as building blocks for all other topics. Includes fundamental financial terms and principles that every other concept depends on.

### 2. Mindset & Goals (MIND)

Money psychology, financial mindset development, goal-setting, and behavioral foundations for building wealth. Covers the mental framework for financial success.

### 3. Budgeting & Cash Flow (BUDG)

Budget creation, expense management, cash flow tracking, and income allocation strategies. The practical day-to-day money management skills.

### 4. Banking & Accounts (BANK)

Checking accounts, savings accounts, online banking, and banking services. Understanding where to keep your money and how banking works.

### 5. Credit & Credit Scores (CRED)

Credit scores, credit reports, credit cards, and credit-building strategies. Everything related to establishing and maintaining good credit.

### 6. Emergency Savings & Safety (SAFE)

Emergency funds, financial safety nets, and sinking funds. Building financial resilience against unexpected events.

### 7. Debt Management (DEBT)

Types of debt, interest rates, debt repayment strategies, and loan management. Strategies for eliminating and managing debt effectively.

### 8. Taxes & Tax Strategy (TAX)

Tax brackets, filing, deductions, credits, and tax-advantaged accounts. Understanding and optimizing your tax situation.

### 9. Investing Fundamentals (INVST)

Core investing concepts including stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs, index funds, diversification, and portfolio management.

### 10. Stock Market (STMKT)

Stock market mechanics, market indices, trading strategies like dollar-cost averaging, and understanding market behavior.

### 11. Retirement Accounts (RETIRE)

IRA accounts, 401(k) plans, employer matching, HSAs, and retirement planning strategies. Tax-advantaged retirement savings vehicles.

### 12. Passive Income (PASSINC)

Dividend investing, rental income, digital products, and other passive income streams. Building income that doesn't require active work.

### 13. Real Estate (REALES)

Home buying, mortgages, renting vs buying analysis, and real estate investment basics.

### 14. Income & Career (INCOME)

Side hustles, freelancing, salary negotiation, and career income optimization strategies.

### 15. Alternative Investments (ALTINV)

Cryptocurrency, blockchain, NFTs, and other non-traditional investment vehicles.

### 16. Insurance & Risk (INSUR)

Health, auto, renters, life, and disability insurance. Understanding risk protection and insurance fundamentals.

### 17. FIRE Movement (FIRE)

Financial independence, early retirement, FI number calculation, savings rate optimization, and different FIRE approaches.

### 18. Behavioral Finance (BEHAV)

Cognitive biases, emotional spending, financial scams, and behavioral patterns that affect financial decisions.

### 19. Estate Planning (ESTATE)

Wills, trusts, beneficiary designations, power of attorney, and protecting assets for the future.

### 20. Generational Wealth (WEALTH)

Building multi-generational wealth, financial literacy education, 529 plans, and family financial values.

### 21. Employer Benefits (EMPBEN)

Employee benefits packages, open enrollment, and understanding total compensation from employers.

### 22. Financial Tools (TOOLS)

Budgeting apps, investment platforms, robo-advisors, financial automation, and building a personal finance technology stack.
